A Novel Black-Start Approach for Inverter-Based Microgrids (Undergraduate Capstone)

2023-2024

This project introduces a novel black-start method for inverter-based microgrids to improve resiliency and rapid power restoration after a blackout. The goal was to design a grid control scheme using multiple grid-forming (GFM) inverters, specifically addressing the instability challenges found in microgrids with high inverter saturation. The system was implemented using a TI C2000 F28379D controller to send PWM signals to a BOOSTXL-3PHGaNINV inverter, successfully forming a stable grid.

This system diagram shows the overall design, consisting of two microgrids with a total of five inverters. Each inverter has its own filter, transformer, and relay, allowing for a staged, bottom-up black-start sequence to restore power reliably.

The black-start sequence (top) shows the timed connection of each inverter. The graphs below show the resulting voltage (left) and current (right) of the initial anchor inverter as it establishes the grid.

Demonstration of overall system performance after synchronization. The final grid output voltage is stable (left), and the output frequency correctly settles at 60 Hz (right).

The control architecture, showing the primary voltage and current control loop (top), the microcontroller system overview (bottom-left), and the droop control scheme for power sharing (bottom-right).

The Texas Instruments C2000 controller and BOOSTXL-3PHGaNINV inverter unit (top), and the full electrical setup including power supplies and measurement equipment (bottom).

Drone Build

2020

Towards the end of highschool I became very interested in drones, deciding to build one myself. I curtailed parts to my own liking, sticking with 4s lipo batteries, eco motors and a stack esc and controller - a mid powered drone with everything a true race FPV drone had equipped. After flying obsesively and crashing frequenly I decided to design disposable 3D printed prtectors for expendages and a stronger antenna mount. As well as going in depth for tuning using getfpv software linked to my controller.

The assembled drone above is the finalized product. After meticulously looking into parts that had a mix of power, durability and price.

After crash landing and running into trees, I decided it would be best to print some disposable parts for the most frequently impacted parts. Prior to these parts I used a lot of hot glue and some plastic drinking straws to hold up antennas.
