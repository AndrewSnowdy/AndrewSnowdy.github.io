About Me

Welcome to my portfolio. I’m an electrical engineering graduate student at Northeastern focused on embedded systems, controls, and real-time robotics. I enjoy working at the boundary where software meets physics—where timing matters, hardware constraints are unavoidable, and systems have to perform reliably outside of simulation.

My work spans control design, state estimation, and embedded development, with experience ranging from multi-robot sensor synchronization and mobile manipulation to whole-body control on legged platforms. I’m especially interested in how complex systems stay stable, coordinated, and predictable under real-world uncertainty.

I enjoy the full engineering loop: modeling and simulation in MATLAB/Simulink Mujoco or Gazebo, implementation in C++ and ROS 2, and hands-on integration, debugging, and testing on hardware. Many of my projects involve deploying systems in uncontrolled environments, where validation and iteration are just as important as design.

This portfolio highlights selected projects that reflect how I approach engineering problems—by building systems that work, measuring their performance, and refining them until they’re robust.
